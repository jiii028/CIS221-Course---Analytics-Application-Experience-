## **‚ùì 3.2. What could have been the reasons why such an event happened? (you could bring in some theory into it). What are the things we have learned so that we can improve from this experience?**

  There are several reasons why these difficulties occurred. First, coding and statistical reasoning both demand significant cognitive effort. According to cognitive load theory, when multiple new skills are learned at once, the brain can feel overloaded. In this course, I had to understand the code, remember the syntax, interpret the output, and apply analytical reasoning‚Äîall at the same time. This naturally made certain tasks feel overwhelming and sometimes too much for my brain to handle.¬†Second, data analysis concepts depend on one another. Without a good understanding of statistics, concepts like regression coefficients, multicollinearity, or model error terms can seem complex. I probably struggled in part because I was still learning that fundamental information. Third, machine learning introduces a level of abstraction and experimentation that can feel less predictable. Unlike regular regression, where rules feel more structured, ML models require constant trial-and-error, tuning, and critical thinking. This can be intimidating and confusing when you‚Äôre still beginning to learn.

  However, despite these challenges, I gained valuable insights:
  * I learned that slowing down and revisiting my notes and notebooks makes advanced concepts easier.
  * I discovered the importance of reading documentation, continuous exploration with code, and validating assumptions rather than trying to rush through steps and depend solely on what we have covered in discussions.
  * I became more aware that analysis is not just about generating outputs but interpreting and understanding the story the data is telling.
  * Most importantly, I believe and have learned that struggling is part of the learning process, and each challenge builds competence, strength, and confidence.
